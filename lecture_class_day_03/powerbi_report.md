# In-class Exercise - Power BI Report

We will continue working on the semantic model for bike order data created during the [in-class exercise last week](https://github.com/kokchun/data_vizualisation_kokchun_giang_de25/blob/main/04_semantic_model/powerbi_semantic_model.md). Continue working on the workspace you used previously for the in-class exercise.

Suppose you are the data engineer of this bike company that owns the bike stores in the data. Your boss would like to see a Power BI report that helps understanding the business. Because your boss would like to see a quick product first before asking you to continue working on it, you don't need to polish your report right now.

> [!TIP]
> Remember that in the industry, a project is often divided into many small tasks. Data engineers, other technical experts and business stakeholders meet frequently to discuss the next steps.

Your boss has, however, given a few requirements on the report. Your report should show:

- the numbers of orders and completed orders
- the numbers of orders by store states
- the distribution of orders by order status
- the numbers of orders and completed orders handled by each staff
- a slicer for your boss to select stores
- a slicer for your boss to select order dates
